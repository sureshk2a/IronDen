When You Hit 24 kg — What To Do Next

1
Increase Reps: Push past the top of the prescribed range (e.g. 12–14 instead of 10). More reps at max load = continued hypertrophy stimulus.
2
Slow the Tempo: 3–4 second eccentric (lowering phase) doubles time under tension. Dramatically harder than adding weight and great for muscle definition.
3
Drop Sets: Last set at 24 kg → immediately drop to 16–18 kg and go to failure. Brutal and very effective for both hypertrophy and fat burning.
4
Add Sets: Go from 3 to 4–5 sets on key lifts. More total volume at the same load still drives meaningful muscle growth.
02
Keys to Your Goal
What separates results from no results
🍽️

Nutrition is 70% of the six-pack

At 80–90 kg, aim for 144–162g protein daily (1.8g per kg bodyweight). Target a 300–500 calorie deficit. No training will reveal abs while eating in a surplus. Track for 2–3 weeks to know where you actually stand.

📈

Progressive Overload — The Only Rule That Matters

The weight roadmap is your north star. Add weight or reps every 1–2 weeks. Without this, you plateau regardless of effort. The body adapts to stress — you must keep increasing it.

😴

Sleep 7–9 Hours — The Highest ROI Tool

Growth hormone and testosterone peak during deep sleep. Skimping tanks both muscle gain and fat loss at the same time. It's free — use it.

🎯

Mind-Muscle Connection

On isolation work (curls, laterals, flys) — intentionally contract the target muscle. Research shows this improves activation by ~20%. Feel the rep, don't just move the weight.

🔄

Deload Every 4–5 Weeks

Drop all weights ~30% and reduce sets for one full week. Prevents overtraining, lets joints recover. You'll come back noticeably stronger. Don't skip this — it's part of the program.

📸

Track Progress the Right Way

Weigh yourself weekly (same time, morning, fasted). Progress photos every 4 weeks — the mirror lies daily, photos show truth. Log your lifts so the roadmap stays on track.
